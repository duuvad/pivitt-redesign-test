The Stakes

Your brand is your case for the levy.

DBP is funded by a Business Improvement District levy. Every business paying into it forms an opinion based on what they see.

Sponsor Attraction
Sponsors choose partners who look credible. A polished brand attracts higher-value sponsors and gives them confidence.
Weak brand = sponsors choosing the next organisation on the list.
Grant Applications
Foundations judge organisational capacity before reading a word of the application. Consistent presentation signals a well-run organisation.
Weak brand = first impression undercuts the application.
Member Confidence
Levy-paying businesses form a running assessment of whether DBP is worth their support.
Weak brand = levy payers questioning their contribution.
Event Attendance
A recognisable brand drives repeat attendance. When SPARK connects to DBP, every event builds equity for the next one.
Weak brand = events treated as one-offs.
Staff & Recruitment
Professional organisations attract better candidates. Brand signals internally as well as externally.
Weak brand = reduced prestige for staff.
Media Coverage
Local journalists feature organisations that look like a headline. Consistent assets make it easy for media to use your materials.
Weak brand = coverage that undersells the organisation.
$14,500 is not a brand spend. It is an investment in the operational credibility of a BID-funded organisation.
