C-Quest

Brand strategy & identity · Cost consultancy

An independent brand for a respected name inside the KEO Group.

C-Quest is a cost consultancy working on mega and giga projects across the Middle East. Established in 2015 and built on more than 60 years of expertise inside the KEO Group, it had grown into one of the most respected names in its field, and was named Cost Consultant of the Year in 2024. The brand had not kept pace. It still read as a department of its parent rather than the independent firm C-Quest had become.

The Challenge

Award-winning work, a brand that read like a subsidiary.

C-Quest was known for delivery, not for a presence of its own. The identity and communications were fragmented, the online footprint was thin, and nothing in the brand signalled the scale, independence or confidence of the business. A firm winning national awards was introducing itself with materials that undersold it.

The Brief

An independent position, and a system the team could run.

Three things had to be true. C-Quest needed a clear, independent position within the KEO Group. The visual and verbal identity had to read as modern and regionally relevant without discarding the legacy it was built on. And the assets had to be ones the internal team could use and maintain with confidence, not files that aged the moment we left.

Strategic Foundation

Precision, transparency and collaboration, made into a brand.

We set the position first: the leading independent cost consultancy, trusted to deliver financial certainty on the most ambitious developments. From there the brand ran on three principles the firm already lived by, precision, transparency and collaboration, carried in one line the whole business could stand behind. Where It Started

The old brand, and the gap it left.

C-Quest had the work but not the presence. The previous site and materials read as generic, with no clear position and little to separate the firm from any other cost consultancy. This is what we started from.

The previous C-Quest website. Capable delivery, undersold at every turn.
The brief, framed as a move from limited recognition to an independent, credible platform.

Brand DNA

Position first, then everything else.

We fixed the position before touching a single visual. C-Quest is the leading independent cost consultancy, trusted to deliver financial certainty on the most ambitious developments. Mission, vision and essence were written so the whole firm could repeat them without a script.

Mission, purpose, vision and essence, set on one page.
The positioning statement that anchors every other decision.

The Brand Pattern

A survey mark, built to frame content.

The identity is drawn from the survey reticle, a symbol of measured precision that fits a cost consultancy. It does one job. It frames content and guides the eye rather than decorating the page. We documented exactly how it sits behind information so it never competes with it.

The mark carries four ideas at once: precision, focus, balance and connection.
The pattern as a working surface, in each of the three core colours.
Applied with a soft gradient so a clear space is always held for the content.

Tested, Not Asserted

We measured the pattern instead of trusting taste.

Most identities ship on opinion. We ran the pattern through heatmap and contrast-grid testing at two strengths. At full strength it competed with the content and readability scored 56. At a quarter strength the hierarchy held and readability rose to 63, with focus unchanged at 78. The pattern ships at 25 percent, the level the data supported.

Pattern at 100 percent. The grid pulls attention off the headline. Clarity 56.
Pattern at 25 percent. Hierarchy intact, same focus, cleaner read. Clarity 63. The version that shipped.

Colour And Type

Two colours, two typefaces, one logic.

CQ Dark Grey anchors the system and CQ Red carries energy and the call to action. A cerulean tint sits outside the core set, reserved for data so the brand never reads as decoration on a results page. Funnel Sans, drawn from the movement of data points, sets the headlines. Aptos keeps long documents readable across Microsoft and the web.

The core palette, with full values for print and screen.
Funnel Sans for headlines, Aptos for body.
Colour applied to data, the place most brands lose discipline.

Imagery System

Real scale, real people.

Two image rules. Projects are shot for clean lines and grandeur, the structures C-Quest works on. People are shot natural and unposed, separated from their background so the individual carries the frame. Together they show a firm that is both serious and human.

Project imagery, framed for form, texture and light.
Portraits with shallow depth, confident and unposed.

One System

Six categories, governed as one.

The brand is not a logo and a colour. It is six connected categories: core identity, stationery, presentation and business tools, the digital platform, the imagery system, and a social and activation kit. Each is built so the internal team can run it without us.

Six categories, mapped as one system.
The system applied across print, screen and environment.

In The World

The brand, applied.

The system was built to be used, not admired. Stationery, business cards, proposal and presentation templates, a new website and a social kit, all carrying the same logic from a board pack to a single post.

Business cards. The pattern, debossed and quiet.
Letterheads and email, consistent in every reply.

Project and sector pages, structured for scale.
An editable social kit the marketing team runs in-house.
The brand at the scale of the work it represents.

The Result

From a name inside KEO to a brand that scales.

C-Quest now introduces itself with the confidence the work always had. The brand is consistent across every channel, the team owns it, and the firm finally looks like the independent leader it is.

One coherent system across every touchpoint.
From traditional quantity surveying to a modern, independent cost consultancy.
Paul Kelly, Managing Director, C-Quest
