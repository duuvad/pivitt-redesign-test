Change is on the Horizon

BMW Park Lane Direct · Automotive

Launching Park Lane Direct, BMW Park Lane's contactless way to buy a car, in eight stages from first look to handover.

8
Stage route
15s
Launch film
10
Page handover pack
5
Channels

The brief

A new way to buy, made to feel effortless.

BMW Park Lane was changing how you buy a car. Park Lane Direct takes you from choosing your BMW to a contactless handover without leaving home. The campaign had to introduce the idea, set out the eight stages and make the whole thing feel like a step forward.

The film

Change is on the horizon.

The launch led with a film: the open road at first light, the car in close, and a single line on the horizon. It set the tone before a word of explanation.

The launch film.

The idea

The route, drawn as a road.

The eight stages were mapped onto the thing the brand is really about: the road ahead. The same route runs through every piece, from the film to the feed.

The eight-stage route, on the road and on its own.

The stages

Eight stages, one at a time.

Each stage was built as its own card, with an icon, a step and a line on what happens, so the route reads at a glance or in full.

From the first look to a contactless handover.

The handover pack

On the road, in hand.

The new identity carried into the BMW Park Lane handover pack, the booklet a customer leaves with: protection, rewards and the road ahead.

Selected pages from the handover pack.

On social

Out in the feed.

The launch ran across social as a carousel and an eight-stage story, plus a standalone post for the new process.

The three-frame carousel and the launch post.

Email and web

Inbox and homepage.

The same horizon carried into email and web headers, so the campaign held from the first touch.

Web and email headers.

The outcome

A launch with a clear road ahead.

Park Lane Direct went live with a campaign that made eight stages feel like one smooth move, and a system built to run across every channel a buyer touches.
